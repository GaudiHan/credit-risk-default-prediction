Key Findings
1
External credit scores are the dominant predictor. EXT_SOURCE_2 is the #1 feature. Applicants scoring below 0.3 have a 15.88% default rate — 3.5× higher than those above 0.6 (4.56%).
2
Debt burden shows a non-linear risk pattern. The highest default rate (8.81%) occurs in the 2x–3.5x credit-to-income band — not at the highest debt levels — suggesting compensating factors like higher income or better credit scores.
3
Employment tenure is a material risk factor. Borrowers employed less than one year default at 10.98% vs. 6.41% for those employed over 5 years — a 1.7× risk multiplier.
4
Model achieves 0.737 ROC-AUC. The Random Forest classifier significantly outperforms random guessing (0.5) and improves on logistic regression baselines, providing reliable default probability estimates.

Methodology
Data
307K applications + bureau history. Missing values imputed; class imbalance handled via class_weight='balanced'.
Features
4 engineered features (debt burden, repayment affordability, employment stability, bureau exposure) added to 122 base features.
Model
Random Forest with 150 estimators, max_depth=10, min_samples_split=100. Evaluation: ROC-AUC on 20% stratified holdout.
Explain
Feature importance analysis identifies EXT_SOURCE_2 as the dominant predictor — interpretable for non-technical credit officers.
Recommendations
● Immediate
Prioritise Low Credit Score Flagging
Applicants with EXT_SOURCE_2 below 0.3 show 15.88% default rates. Implement enhanced review or additional verification for this segment.
● Medium-Term
Deploy Model Score as Decision Supplement
Integrate Random Forest default probability (ROC-AUC 0.737) alongside bureau checks. Target high-recall threshold to capture 80% of defaults.
● Strategic
Investigate Medium DTI Segment
The 2x–3.5x credit-to-income band shows peak default risk (8.81%). Further segmentation analysis could uncover specific risk drivers within this group.
